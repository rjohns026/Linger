Product Roadmap — As of February 2026

What we're building and when.

Features listed in rough build order within each phase — earlier items ship first.

01

The Knowledge Layer

Churches upload their sermon archive. Users explore it with AI.

User accounts Individual and church admin accounts. Church selects and manages their congregation.
Sermon archive upload Churches upload existing sermon audio/video. Transcribed and structured with scripture references and themes.
Browse and search Searchable sermon history by date, series, topic, or scripture reference.
AI sermon summaries Every archived sermon gets a structured summary — main points, references, key themes.
AI chat Ask questions, trace themes across sermons, get references, explore connections.
Reading plans AI-generated devotionals and study guides built from the church's teaching history.
02

Historical Theology

Add centuries of public domain theological content alongside church sermons.

Public domain corpus Spurgeon, Augustine, Edwards, and others — ingested and connected to sermon content.
Cross-referencing AI surfaces connections between your church's teaching and the broader theological tradition.
Theological exploration Ask Linger how a topic has been addressed across centuries — not just at your church.
03

The Sunday Experience

Personal notes during the service. Church uploads content after.

In-service note-taking Personal annotations, bookmarks, and thoughts captured during the sermon.
Slide photography Users photograph slides during the service. OCR extracts text and references.
Church post-service upload Church uploads sermon video, audio, and slides after the service for processing.
AI synthesis Personal notes merged with the church's uploaded content into one polished document.
Handwritten note capture Photo of journal page → OCR → woven into the synthesized notes.
04

Modern Resources

Books, podcasts, courses, and contemporary voices.

Licensed content Modern commentary, books, podcasts, and courses integrated into the knowledge layer.
Resource recommendations AI suggests relevant books, courses, and podcasts based on sermon content and user interests.
Extended learning paths Multi-week study plans that weave together sermons, theology, and modern resources.

Not yet sequenced

Live audio capture Real-time recording from church mixing console or phone mic.
Real-time transcription Live notes during the service rather than post-service delivery.
Android iOS first. Android timing depends on adoption.
Small group tools Shared notes, discussion guides, group reading plans.
